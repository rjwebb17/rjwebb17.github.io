Biography

I study how people collaborate with intelligent systems, with an emphasis on transparency, trust, and equitable outcomes. My work bridges theory and practice, translating research into tools that support learning, health, and civic life.

I advise graduate students in the Interactive Computing Lab and teach courses on machine learning, human-computer interaction, and ethics in computing.

Research Highlights
Adaptive Learning Systems: Designing AI tutors that respond to student needs while maintaining transparency.
Responsible AI: Auditing models for fairness and developing governance frameworks for public-sector deployments.
Community Partnerships: Collaborating with local organizations to co-create data-driven services.
